About the Nobel Prize

Understanding the history, purpose and global impact of the Nobel Prize

How and When the Nobel Prize Started

The Nobel Prize was established through the will of Alfred Nobel, a Swedish inventor and industrialist. According to his will, his wealth was to be used to create a series of prizes for those who had made the most important contributions to mankind.

The first Nobel Prizes were awarded in the year 1901. Since then, the awards have been presented annually to honour excellence, innovation and dedication to improving the world.

Fields in Which the Nobel Prize Is Awarded

The Nobel Prize is awarded in the following fields:

  • Physics
  • Chemistry
  • Physiology or Medicine
  • Literature
  • Peace
  • Economic Sciences

Each category recognizes outstanding achievements and discoveries that have had a significant impact on human knowledge and society.

Nominations Process

Nominations for the Nobel Prize are submitted every year by qualified individuals such as university professors, previous laureates and selected experts. Each field follows a strict and confidential evaluation process.

The nominated candidates are carefully reviewed by specialized committees, and only the most deserving contributions are selected for the final award.

Nobel Prize Winners

Nobel Prize winners, also known as Nobel Laureates, are individuals or organizations whose work has significantly advanced science, culture, peace and human development.

Each laureate is recognized not only for a single achievement but also for the lasting influence of their work on society and future generations.

Our Website Purpose

This website is created as an academic and informational platform to help students, researchers and the general public learn about the Nobel Prize, its history, award categories, nomination process and distinguished winners.

The goal of this portal is to provide reliable, well-structured and easy-to-read information while encouraging curiosity and appreciation for human achievements.
